Rocket Project




Designed a model rocket with an electrical circuit to take gps and data measurements over duration of the flight.

The first picture is a rocket launch, the second picture is the original prototype circuit. The circuit contains an accelerometer, altimeter, and gps module.

The model rocket was created with 3D printed parts, the onboard electronics circuit, and an estes rocket engine.

Physics calculations were used to find the estimated max altitude using the known mass and estes engine impulse force.

Rocket Vectoring Model Prototype


Prototype designed to stabilize rocket take offs and landings. Current modeling iterations are square because they are easier to design during the initial phases of prototyping.

Inspiration taken from previous BPS space and SpaceX rockets.

Star Pointer



Insert your coordinates, time and the celestial object, and it should track the selected object over time.

The math is mostly simple, but extremely tedious.

From least to most difficult to track, the order is:
1 - stars,
2 - sun,
3 - moon,
4 - planets,
5 - and satelites like the ISS.

Otherwise, the simplest solution is to use a arduino library from github that automatically calculates the position of each object for you, which the values can be sent directly to the stepper motors on the device.

Senior Design: Solar Water Heater


Our Senior Design Project was to heat water from concentrated solar radiation which would be used for desalination purposes.

We designed this project from scratch, going through the design process with a top down approach. We gained experience through designing, manufacturing and testing our final design.

Final temperture reached 140F during testing, would have achieved higher, but our pond pump thermal throttled at this higher temperture.
