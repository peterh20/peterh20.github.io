Senior Design: Solar Water Heater


Our Senior Design Project was to heat water from concentrated solar radiation which would be used for desalination purposes.

We designed this project from scratch, going through the design process with a top down approach. We gained experience through designing, manufacturing and testing our final design.

Final temperture reached 140F during testing, would have achieved higher, but our pond pump thermal throttled at this higher temperture.

Star Pointer



Insert your coordinates, time and the celestial object, and it should track the selected object over time.

The math is mostly simple, but extremely tedious.

From least to most difficult to track, the order is:
1 - stars,
2 - sun,
3 - moon,
4 - planets,
5 - and satelites like the ISS.

Otherwise, the simplest solution is to use a arduino library from github that automatically calculates the position of each object for you, which the values can be sent directly to the stepper motors on the device.

Lego Robotic Etch-A-Sketch



Inspired by 2D and 3D printers and boredom, I wanted to build something that could write and draw.


Using Lego EV3 motors and sensors, I created a lego robot that can be programmed consistantly to write words and draw pictures. Two motors positioned the X and Y directions, while a third motor was the pen up-and-down movement.


The robot would slide back-and-forth in the X direction, while the dolly (the thing on the flat tracks) moved left-to-right in the perpendicular Y direction. To start each series of drawings, the robot would move to a specfic corner until the touch sensors were pressed, which would signal that the robot was in position. This was the (0,0) for the robot.


Creating consistancy was difficult without the use of any gear tracks, so I had to play around with different motor powers and speeds. To avoid sliding, I added batteries to function as weights to increase the traction that the wheels had.


The bottom picture shows a consistancy test, where I tried to measure how precise the robot could replicate drawing the same cubes. Each drawn cube is an inch wide, so the robot was able to consistantly draw to a precision of 1/8 of an inch, which I think is pretty good for something made out of legos.

Electric Gocart


After wanting to build my own gocart, I created my own using a free abandoned kids electric gocart toy that I found up the road from my house.


After charging the battery to make sure the motors work, I spent a few weeks of tearing most of the front of the original gocart toy apart. Eventually, only the back frame of the cart remained.


On another random day, my neighbors were throwing away their own wooden push-cart, and I decided to use that as the front steering. I cut the woodoen board so it could be bolted to the back frame of the cart. With the front and back ends connected, I created a pvc pipe steering wheel and a little dashboard that the buttons were glued to.


The cart only had a speed of a brisk walking pace, and could only circle around my neghborhood block, but it was fun to create and my brothers had a fun time riding it down the sidewalk. After my discovery of powerful E-bike batteries, eventually I want to create a cart that is powered by several and goes very fast.

Dog Wheelchair Harness



My aunt and uncle's dog was getting old so they got bought a dog wheelchair, but since the wheels were too big, they got caught on corners when the dog was walking around inside the house. I wanted to build a set of indoor wheels that wouldn't get stuck.


I cut and glued a pvc pipe frame that would fit inside the structure of the dog wheelchair, and I attached wheels to the bottom of the frame. The frame obviously needed to be the correct height, but also the correct length so the hind legs would not get stuck under the pipe which was providing structure.

Infrared Camera




When the infrared filter is taken out of a camera, the camera is able to see infrared light, which is invisible to the human eye. When used in the dark, IR light illuminates the area on camera. So, using an infrared LED from an old TV remote, I soldered a simple LED-resistor-battery circuit together, creating an IR flashlight.


In regular light, we see different wavelengths of light, which produces the color we see. On camera, glossy photos look completely white, and striped shirts made of the same cloth look like there are no stripes. This is because IR light is only one wavelength of light, and is reflecting that light off of one singular homogeneous material.

Pellet Gun




I modified an old nerf gun with PVC pipe and other tubes to create a pellet gun that could puncture paper. The barrel was made with layers of pvc pipe and tape which would create a snug and straight fit. The most difficult part was securing the pellet inside of the bolt without the pellet falling out if it was pointed downwards. A little paper was used inside the bolt to create enough friction to hold the pellet in place without it falling out.
